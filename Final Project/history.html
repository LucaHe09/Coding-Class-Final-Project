History of Miami

Miami has an intreresting history. Spanish first found the native american village of Mayaimi in the 16th century. The name “Mayaimi” means “big water” or “sweet water.” They gave up the territory to Great Britain in 1763, but got it back in 1783. The United States bought Florida from Spain in 1821. Fort Dallas was built there in 1836 for the Seminole Wars, a war between the United States and the Seminole Indians, ending in the U.S. gaining the Seminiole’s territory. People such as Julia D. Tuttle, also known as “Mother of Miami,” started to settle in Florida after the war.
